For the 99% who are done begging

They rule because we obey. We stop, they crack.

Not a petition. Not a lifestyle. A movement for organised noncooperation.

A tiny elite treats people, law, and the living world as disposable. People Before Power turns fear, anger, and isolation into disciplined public refusal — blockades, strikes, boycotts, walkouts, and civil disobedience that raises the cost of elite impunity.

Join the front line Read the manifesto

No worship of wealth. No obedience to impunity. No faith in a system built to protect the few.

Our line

  • They are violent. Climate collapse, policing, extraction, prisons, war, and elite abuse are not side effects. They are how rule is maintained.
  • We keep the machine alive. The 1% only look untouchable because the 99% keep the streets, wires, hospitals, logistics, and institutions running.
  • We fight without becoming them. Public, strategic, disruptive nonviolence widens participation while denying power the spectacle it wants.

Diagnosis

This is not normal politics

We are told to be patient, polite, and realistic. Meanwhile a tiny ruling minority concentrates wealth, builds surveillance, bankrolls hard-right projects, protects abusers, and treats whole populations as disposable. The point of this campaign is to tear away the language of normality and name the system for what it is: organised violence with good branding.

They are violent

Power is enforced

Not just with police and prisons, but with debt, exhaustion, precarity, ecological collapse, and the threat that resistance will cost you everything.

We are trained

Obedience is manufactured

People are not passive by nature. We are pressured into isolation, survival reflexes, and the lie that nothing changes unless the powerful allow it.

We hold the real leverage

The system runs through us

Every city, supply chain, service, office, terminal, school, and network depends on ordinary people continuing to cooperate with an unjust order.

They hold guns and paper wealth. We hold bodies, streets, wires, switches, care, labour, and time. When we withdraw cooperation together, their inevitability starts to look like theatre.

Method

Turn obedience into disruption

We are not here to ask the 1% to behave. We are here to make business-as-usual harder to sustain. That means public organisation, clear targets, visible campaigns, and tactics that ordinary people can actually carry out: coordinated refusal, strategic slowdown, labour power, boycotts, blockades, occupations, and civil disobedience.

Targeted

Hit legitimacy

Expose the gap between how elite power presents itself and what it actually protects — extraction, impunity, and disposable lives.

Collective

Raise the social cost

Move from private outrage to organised refusal that jams routines, interrupts flows, and shows people they are not alone.

Disciplined

Stay nonviolent

Not because the system deserves courtesy, but because disciplined mass nonviolence scales faster, includes more people, and protects communities from the logic of war.

Escalation

From first step to shutdown

  1. Break the silence

    Sign. Share. Show up once. Say openly what the system is and stop acting like this is somebody else’s fight.

  2. Win a visible fight

    Join a local campaign with a sharp target. Build confidence by turning pressure into something public, concrete, and contagious.

  3. Scale disruption

    Move toward coordinated blockades, strikes, slow-downs, and refusals that make it impossible to pretend consent is still intact.

Red lines

Hard line. No drift.

The system will call any serious resistance extreme. That is why our own lines stay clear. We are trying to build power for human beings, not copy the contempt of the people we are fighting.

  • No random targets. We confront systems, institutions, and concentrations of power — not scapegoats or civilians.
  • No macho violence. No weapons, no terror, no fantasies of purification through blood.
  • No secret war. Our strength is organised, public disruption that ordinary people can join and defend.
  • No hero cults. The point is not new rulers. The point is collective leverage, dignity, and a livable world.

Safety

Anonymity and safety

Protect identity by default

For anonymity and safety, avoid mobile phones during sensitive coordination and use a character name or alias even in face-to-face meetings. Keep real names, contact details, and other identifying information private unless absolutely necessary, and use offline, low-tech methods for coordination whenever possible.

Risk

Ask the hard questions

Is this legal?

Some actions are legal. Some are civil disobedience. The point is not to hide that fact, but to prepare for it together and make repression visible when it comes.

Will I be safe?

No honest movement can promise safety. What we can promise is preparation, mutual support, de-escalation, legal awareness, and a refusal to let people face consequences alone.

Why not answer violence with violence?

Because copying elite violence shrinks the movement, isolates communities, and hands power the spectacle it wants. Disciplined nonviolence is not softness. It is strategy.
