# People Before Power - Manifesto

## 1. We live under organised violence, not "normal politics"

We are told that we live in democracies, that history bends towards justice, that "the system" may be slow but basically rational. We are told that if we just vote, consume, recycle, send polite emails and behave ourselves, things will improve.

Look around.

A tiny global minority - call it the 1% - concentrates wealth and power on a scale humanity has never seen. Their lifestyles and investments drive climate breakdown. Their decisions lock millions into precarity while private fortunes explode. Their wars, borders and policing treat whole populations as disposable.

We must start from this: **what we face is not a neutral policy debate but a structure of organised violence**. It is slow violence (climate, debt, exhaustion) and fast violence (police, prisons, drones), and it is wielded deliberately by those who benefit.

### The Epstein system: abuse as a privilege of power

The Jeffrey Epstein case exposes this system in its purest form. For years, a convicted sex offender ran a trafficking network that targeted girls and young women while moving comfortably among billionaires, politicians, academics and media figures.

This is not gossip about private lives. It is **structural violence laid bare**: a world in which the bodies of women and children are treated as disposable perks of membership in a protected caste, and where institutions often look away as long as power is preserved.

---

## 2. Why the 99% look passive

If the 1% are so few, why do they seem untouchable? Why do the 99% - the people who work, care, build, fix, drive, code, grow and heal - so often appear indifferent, opportunistic, fragmented?

Because people are not cowards by nature. They are **threatened**.

Decades of structural violence train people to shrink their horizons. You are taught to keep your head down because those who raise it get crushed, or at least ruined. You learn that "nothing changes anyway" and that you should cling to whatever tiny advantage you have.

What looks like mass opportunism is often **a survival reflex**. When people choose the small deal, the small escape, the small betrayal, they are navigating a world where the powerful have shown again and again that they are prepared to ruin lives and even destroy planetary conditions rather than lose control.

When you see year after year that men with money and connections can be named in sworn testimony, appear in official files, and still keep their platforms, companies and media empires, you learn a brutal lesson: **there is one law for them and another for everyone else**.

So we refuse to blame the 99% as if they were lazy or selfish. We blame the system that makes courage look suicidal and obedience look like the only sane choice.

### We stay sharp, we do not bow

The men at the top have shown that they are willing to use brutal, even barbaric violence - against bodies, against societies, against the living world - to defend their position. From climate destruction and war to trafficking networks, everything points in the same direction: **they will burn what they cannot own.**

That is exactly why we cannot surrender to their threats or to the myth of their invincibility. Fear is their first weapon, not their last.

**Our task is to stay sharp and smart under pressure**: to look directly at their violence and still refuse to crawl, to organise in ways that make people feel less alone and more capable together, and to turn shared fear into shared courage instead of private despair.

Collective power is not a feeling we wait for; it is something we build by acting as if it were already true - and then proving, step by step, that it is.

---

## 3. Theory becomes force when it seizes the 99%

As long as our understanding of this order - of the 1%, of structural violence, of the climate emergency - remains a private opinion or a niche subculture, it is harmless to those who rule. It is just talk.

But when millions of people start from the same diagnosis, shared understanding becomes **shared action**: strikes, boycotts, blockades, stay-aways, occupations and refusals.

Our task is not only to be right. Our task is to **turn a clear analysis of the 1% and their violence into a shared story that ordinary people act on together**.

### The 1% are not neutral - they are building a hard-right future

The network exposed by elite abuse files intersects with a broader hard-right, techno-authoritarian project promoted and funded by billionaire circles.

The point is not a single master conspiracy. The point is that the same families of men, money and machines keep showing up: at the heart of abuse networks, at the core of far-right politics, and at the cutting edge of surveillance and military technology.

---

## 4. The temptation of violence - and why we refuse to mirror them

When you are crushed by systems of absolute violence, the desire for counter-violence is real.

We understand that temptation. But if we answer by embracing the same logic - if we say "anything is justified now" - we will build new systems of domination on top of old ruins.

People Before Power takes a different line:

- We **name** the violence of the 1% as violence - economic, racial, ecological, military.
- We **refuse** to answer with their tools: massacres, terror, targeted killing, sacrificial logics.
- We commit instead to **maximally disruptive, strategically organised nonviolence** that can make their order ungovernable without sacrificing human beings the way they do.

Nonviolence for us is not passivity. It is choosing to fight with methods that expand the space of life and dignity instead of shrinking it.

---

## 5. Our method: stop their world without becoming them

Nonviolent resistance has never been about staying polite. It is about **organising power**: economic power, labour power, the power of bodies in streets and squares, and the power of public refusal.

For People Before Power, this means:

- We aim at **the 1% and the systems that protect them**, not random civilians.
- Our tactics range from small local wins to **mass street blockades and strikes in capital cities**.
- We are explicit that some actions cross legal lines, and we prepare for repression.
- We draw a hard line against physical violence, weapons and humiliation of individuals.

We are not asking the 1% to behave. We are **forcing a change in the cost of continuing as they are** by withdrawing cooperation.

---

## 6. From small wins to shutting down capitals

Huge crises paralyse people. Lasting movements are built through cumulative, visible wins.

1. **Break the silence**
   People take one low-risk step: sign, share, show up once.
2. **Win a visible fight**
   Local groups stop a specific contract, project or corrupt deal.
3. **Move to mass disruption**
   Coordinated strikes, stay-aways and street blockades in capital cities that interrupt political and financial operations.

Each victory is evidence that the 1% only rule because the 99% are treated as raw material, not because they are gods.

---

## 7. Our red lines: nonviolence and dignity

Whatever we do, some will call us extremists and criminals. That is exactly why we keep our own lines clear:

- We will not kill or torture for a cause.
- We will not target random civilians or scapegoats.
- We will not build new hierarchies that replicate contempt.
- We will not glorify war, macho heroics or sacrificial death.

Our discipline is not respectability politics. It is **a weapon**. It denies the 1% the images they need to justify open war on their own populations.

---

## Civil Disobedience: Our Line in the Sand

Civil disobedience becomes a duty the moment "law" openly turns into a weapon for a tiny class instead of a shield for society.

When a state brands people protesting an ICE detention center as an "antifa terror cell" and locks them away in Texas for a combined 450 years, while many Capitol attackers walk away with lighter sentences or political rehabilitation, we are not dealing with "errors in the system" anymore - we are dealing with a justice machine that knows exactly whose side it is on, and who it wants to crush.

Civil disobedience is the conscious decision not to play along with that fraud. It is not a polite form or a hashtag - it is the open, nonviolent breaking of rules that protect injustice: blocking streets, disrupting routines, interrupting money flows until it becomes visible what this system really defends - deportation prisons, surveillance, militarized policing and impunity for authoritarian networks around billionaires and their toxic media ecosystems.

Where law has turned into injustice, obedience is nothing but submission. Choosing non-compliance is not a "radical slip"; it is an act of self-respect - a willingness to risk arrest, repression and smear campaigns in order to say, publicly and clearly, that the real crime is being committed where power is combined with impunity.

People Before Power is an invitation to draw that line. Not to secret violence, not to blind chaos, but to clear, organised disruption: blockades in capital cities, economic slow-downs, refusal to cooperate with a system that protects the 1% and disciplines the 99% as if we were a passive audience. We do not show up to destroy the idea of law; we show up to prove that where "law" has become a facade for an unjust order, civil disobedience is not a hobby of the outraged - it is the sharpest form of honesty society still has.

---

## 9. A call to the 99%

This manifesto is not a theory exercise. It is a **call to take sides**.

If you are part of the 99% - worker or unemployed, citizen or undocumented, North or South - you are already paying the price for their decisions. You have already been told that nothing can be done. That is a lie.

People Before Power exists to:

- Tell the truth about the violence of this system.
- Turn that truth into **material force** through organised refusals, blockades and strikes.
- Hold a nonviolent line that lets us fight without becoming the thing we are fighting.

You do not have to be ready for the biggest risk tomorrow. Your first step can be small. But it has to be **a step** - out of isolation, out of resignation, out of the story that says the 1% are all-powerful and the 99% are spectators.

They are a minority sitting on a throne made of our work, our time, our attention and our fear. When we move, the throne cracks.

**People Before Power is an invitation to move.**
