Purpose

The frontpage and manifesto position the movement as public, organised, and explicitly nonviolent, with red lines against random targets, weapons, and secret-war logic. This page keeps that line by focusing on visible participation, local outreach, and RSVP-style commitment rather than hidden operational planning. [file:4][file:5][file:3]

Schedule

Upcoming activity windows

The current site emphasizes a ladder from small first steps to larger coordinated action, so the schedule below is structured in the same way: low-barrier outreach, public assemblies, training, and city presence. It is a page for commitment and visibility, not covert logistics. [file:4][file:5]

Week 1

Street conversation hour

Format: Leafleting, canvassing, visible presence Timing: Wednesday, 18:00–19:00 Radius: Near your district or town center

A first step for people willing to speak with neighbors, hand out material, and invite others into the campaign.

Week 2

Banner and witness gathering

Format: Public square gathering Timing: Saturday, 15:00–16:30 Radius: Central public meeting point

A visible, disciplined public presence for signs, conversation, photography, and outreach without confrontation.

Week 3

Training and role prep

Format: Briefing circle Timing: Tuesday, 19:30–20:30 Radius: Local indoor or online meeting

A prep session for de-escalation, legal awareness, documentation, first aid, and public messaging roles.

Roles

Ground rules

Keep the page aligned with the campaign

The current homepage says the movement fights through organised disruption while staying nonviolent, and the manifesto repeats a hard line against killing, random targets, weapons, humiliation, and secret-war thinking. Any participation page should preserve that framework by centering public witness, outreach, lawful assembly, role signup, and explicit de-escalation expectations. [file:4][file:5][file:3]
